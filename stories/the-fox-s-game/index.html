The Fox's Game

Preamble

In feudal Japan, where social conventions reign supreme, loneliness can become a prison more oppressive than the thickest walls. For Yuki, a young noblewoman, days flow with the regularity of an hourglass in her luxurious Kyōto residence, each grain representing another moment of isolation.

The arrival of a mysterious strategy game, a gift from her absent husband, will change her existence. What begins as a simple pastime quickly becomes a consuming obsession, transforming her nights into a theater of shadows where reality and imagination merge.

When her husband finally returns, he discovers an unrecognizable woman, prisoner of a spiral whose rules she alone seems to know. His quest to save her will lead him to the heart of an enigma where ancestral traditions and unspoken desires mingle, revealing that some games, once started, can no longer be stopped.

A captivating story about the fine line between passion and obsession, and about the dangers that await us when we seek to escape our solitude at any cost.

Selected Passages

Yuki's Deterioration

The mansion, once a symbol of order and elegance, was unrecognizable. Signs of neglect were everywhere: empty tea cups and scattered fans littered the tables, while delicate clothing was left abandoned. The house, once full of life and laughter, now bore the stigmata of deep disorder.

Determined to find a solution, he set out to seek help. He thought of an exorcist monk renowned for his wisdom and knowledge of ailments of the soul. Perhaps, he hoped, this wise man could free Yuki from the grip of this strange curse that seemed tied to the game of ōgi.

The Temple Purification Ritual

The husband walked quickly toward the temple, holding the ōgiban firmly under his arm. He felt a growing anxiety invade him, as if something was pursuing him in the darkness. He turned around several times, but saw nothing but trees and the river glittering under the moon. His mind was tormented by thoughts of his wife, plunged into a dark obsession because of this ancient game.

We will purify this with sacred fire, declared the priest in a gentle but firm voice, guiding the husband to a large pyre where offerings and prayers were already being consumed. The flames danced toward the night sky, as if they linked the earthly world to that of spirits.
