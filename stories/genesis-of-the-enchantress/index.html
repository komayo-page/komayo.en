Genesis of the Enchantress

Preamble

In Edo period Japan, where shadows dance with lantern light and stories are whispered in the darkness of moonless nights, an ancestral ritual takes place: the Hyakumonogatari Kaidankai. This ceremony of one hundred ghost stories, a centuries-old tradition, invites participants to share their most troubling tales while extinguishing, one by one, the hundred andons that illuminate the room.

On this night, in a traditional house with rice paper walls, a group has gathered to perpetuate this tradition. The lanterns cast their flickering lights on attentive faces, creating a ballet of shadows that seem to dance to the rhythm of the participants' heartbeats.

As the eighty-fourth andon continues to burn peacefully, a woman takes the floor. Her name is Mayoko, and she is about to share a tale that dates back to the Kamakura era. A story that begins with a simple game, born in a remote village, and finds its conclusion in the court of a powerful gokenin.

In the growing darkness of the room, as incense burns slowly and shadows lengthen, the assembly holds its breath. For in this Japan where yokai prowl in the darkness and magic permeates every corner of existence, the simplest stories can hide the deepest secrets.

And tonight, as the eighty-fourth flame flickers gently, a voice rises to tell a story that crosses ages, a story of passion, honor, and destiny.

Selected Excerpts

The Birth of Ōgi

"My home village was a small, remote place," she begins, her voice tinged with sweetness and wonder. "As an only child, I was a bundle of energy, always seeking creativity and stimulation. My parents often said they had to constantly feed my eager mind."

"At six years old, my parents enrolled me in a shōgi club. And at eight, my father gave me a very special gift." With a graceful movement, she opens her bag and takes out an object that immediately captures the assembly's attention: a board that evokes a chess game, but with an aura of antiquity and mystery. She places it before her with almost ceremonial care.

Elegance and Anticipation Before the Duel

"A grand duel was organized in the gokenin's courtyard," she begins. "For this event, the staff ensured I was prepared with an elegance worthy of a princess."

"I was dressed in a deep midnight blue kimono, evoking the mysterious essence of night. The fabric, adorned with delicate floral patterns and mythological representations such as dragons and phoenixes, symbolized both strength and rebirth. The patterns, woven in gold and silver thread, subtly sparkled in the light, adding a touch of refinement."
